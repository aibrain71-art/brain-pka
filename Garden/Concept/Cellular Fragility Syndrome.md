Cellular Fragility Syndrome is a newly identified nutritional deficiency syndrome related to low levels of pentadecanoic acid (C15:0). This deficiency can lead to fragile cell membranes, potentially causing accelerated aging and increasing the risk of metabolic, heart, and liver conditions [2, 3, 5]. It is associated with ferroptosis, a form of cell death, and may affect a significant portion of the population [1, 2, 5].
